\subsection*{$\Delta N_e$ between X chromosomes and autosomes}

Assume 10 individuals progress to the next generation and compare two different mating systems. 
An unbalanced sex ratio can occurr in species with high sexual selection, where males are under intense competition and only very few successfully mate.
$\Delta N_e$ between X and A can differ due to the sex ratio of mating individuals (X chromosomes shown in red, Y in blue). 

\vspace{0.25cm}

\begin{minipage}{.5\textwidth}
    \begin{center}
    \textbf{Balanced sex ratio:} $\frac{\male}{\female} = \frac{5}{5}$
    \end{center}
\end{minipage}%
\begin{minipage}{.5\textwidth}
    \begin{center}
    \textbf{Unbalanced sex ratio:} $\frac{\male}{\female} = \frac{1}{9}$
    \end{center}
\end{minipage}

\begin{figure}[H]
    \centering
    \includegraphics[width=0.75\linewidth]{deltaNe.png}
    \label{SIfig:synteny_BsilAobt}
\end{figure}

\vspace{0.25cm}
\noindent
Count X chromosomes and Autosomes that are passed on to the next generation (two X chromosomes for each female and one for each male, and two A for both males and females)
\vspace{-0.25cm}

\begin{minipage}{.5\textwidth}
    \begin{center}
    \begin{align*}
        \female (5 \cdot 2) \: + \: \male (5 \cdot 1) \; &= \; 15 \text{X}\\
        \female (5 \cdot 2) \: + \: \male (5 \cdot 2) \; &= \; 20 \text{A}
    \end{align*}
    \end{center}
\end{minipage}%
\begin{minipage}{.5\textwidth}
    \begin{center}
    \begin{align*}
        \female (9 \cdot 2) \: + \male (1 \cdot 1) \; &= \; 19 \text{X}\\
        \female (5 \cdot 2) \: + \male (5 \cdot 2) \; &= \; 20 \text{A}
    \end{align*}
    \end{center}
\end{minipage}

\vspace{0.25cm}
\noindent
Calculate difference in $N_e$ between X and A based on chromosome counts:
\vspace{-0.25cm}

\begin{minipage}{.5\textwidth}
    \begin{center}
    \begin{align*}
        N_e \text{X} \; &= \; N_e \text{A} \cdot 0.75\\
        \frac{4}{3} N_e \text{X} \; &= \; N_e \text{A}
    \end{align*}
    \end{center}
\end{minipage}%
\begin{minipage}{.5\textwidth}
    \begin{center}
    \begin{align*}
        N_e \text{X} \; &= \; N_e \text{A} \cdot 0.95\\
        \frac{20}{19} N_e \text{X} \; &= \; N_e \text{A}
    \end{align*}
    \end{center}
\end{minipage}
